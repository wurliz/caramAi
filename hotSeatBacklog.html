Hot Seat — Game Design Document

v0.1 — Technology Proof of Concept

3D Simulator · PC 2 devs · part-time Passion project AI-powered NPC
Player fantasy
A hostage negotiator who unravels the fractured psychology of a desperate individual — one careful word at a time — while piecing together the truth from the evidence strewn around them.
Core mechanics
1
InvestigatePlayer freely looks around a crime-scene-style room using mouse/WASD. Objects are highlighted on hover. Clicking an object adds a clue to the player's dossier.
2
SpeakPlayer types (or selects prompt starters) into a phone/radio interface. An AI-powered hostage taker responds in real time, with personality, emotional state, and memory of what's been said.
3
Balance tensionEvery response from the player affects a hidden Tension meter. Push too hard or say the wrong thing — tension spikes. Find the right angle — tension drops. The meter is not shown directly; the hostage taker's voice and body language are the feedback.
4
Unlock contextEvidence found in the room unlocks new conversation topics. Some doors only open when the player has found the relevant clue. This rewards investigation and creates a satisfying "I know something about you" moment.
5
ResolveSituation ends in one of several outcomes: peaceful surrender, partial de-escalation, breakdown, or failure. Outcomes are determined by accumulated tension, clues unlocked, and key conversational moments — not a single binary choice.
Win / lose conditions
De-escalation (win)
Tension falls below threshold and player has unlocked enough context to offer a credible "out." Hostage taker stands down. Hostages released unharmed.
Escalation (lose)
Tension maxes out. Hostage taker becomes unreachable. Scene ends badly. Player gets a brief post-mortem showing which moments tipped the balance.
Stalemate (partial)
Time limit reached with tension mid-range. SWAT called in. Morally ambiguous outcome — player must sit with it. Unlocks a different narrative epilogue.
Breakthrough (bonus)
Player uncovers the root cause and addresses it directly. Hostage taker breaks down emotionally and surrenders. Rare — requires finding specific evidence chain.
The AI hostage taker — design spec
Character state machine
The AI is prompted with a fixed persona (backstory, motive, emotional state, speech patterns) and a dynamic context block that updates with: current tension level, clues the player has found, conversation history, and time elapsed.
Tension influence
The system prompt instructs the AI to shift tone based on tension tier: calm/guarded at low tension, defensive and volatile at mid, irrational and threatening at high. This is behavioural guidance, not a rigid script.
Memory
Conversation history passed in full per call (within token budget). AI explicitly told to reference earlier statements and catch contradictions. Creates the feeling of talking to a real, attentive person.
Cheap model strategy (addressing open question)
This game is well-suited to a smaller model. The hostage taker has a fixed, well-defined persona — this dramatically reduces the model's creative burden. A tightly written system prompt with clear emotional tiers and speech quirks will do most of the heavy lifting. Recommended approach: prototype with GPT-4o-mini or Claude Haiku 3.5, evaluate for believability, only escalate to a larger model if clearly needed. Each exchange is short — cost per session should be manageable.
Art direction
Cel-shaded neo-noir — "living graphic novel"
High-contrast chiaroscuro lighting. Neon cyan, magenta, amber against near-black environments. Heavy ink outline on all geometry. 1980s urban decay — peeling wallpaper, flickering fluorescents, rain-streaked glass. The room should feel like a panel from a Frank Miller comic that the player can walk around inside.
UI language
Phone/radio interface rendered as a physical prop in-world. Dossier is a literal folder on the desk. Tension indicated through environmental cues: lighting gets redder, audio crackles more, rain intensifies.
Audio direction
AI voice optionally synthesised (ElevenLabs or similar). Ambient: rain, distant sirens, muffled crowd. Score: minimalist jazz-influenced tension underscore that reacts to the conversation state.
Engagement strategy (addressing open question)
The "feeling stuck" risk is real. Mitigations built into the design:

Nudge system: If no new evidence is examined for 90 seconds, a subtle environmental cue draws attention (object glints, phone crackles with a hint). Non-diegetic hints available as an opt-in.

AI as compass: The hostage taker is prompted to occasionally drop emotional breadcrumbs — references to names, places, grievances — that implicitly point the player toward relevant evidence.

Soft time pressure: A loose time limit (30–45 min in-game) prevents indefinite stalling without feeling punishing. A visible clock on the wall adds dread without a hard game-over countdown.

Replayability: Because the AI response is generative, each playthrough conversation is unique even in the same scenario. Players are encouraged to try different angles.

Won't have (v1)

Risks
AI reliabilityModel may break character or give nonsensical responses. Mitigation: robust system prompt, fallback canned responses for failure states, low temperature setting.
API costMonitor token usage per session. Use Haiku/mini-class models first. Set a session token cap with graceful degradation.
Player engagementConversation fatigue if character feels repetitive. Mitigation: rich persona, varied emotional states, environmental storytelling carrying equal weight.
Scope creepTwo-person part-time team. Strict Must Have discipline — ship one great scenario before expanding.
